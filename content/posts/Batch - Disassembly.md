**Fantastic appreciation from Siemens Fonden** to one our [Remanufactory](https://remanufactory.dtu.dk/) BSc students. In his thesis, Mikkel has worked on an initial batch-disassembly prototype (incl. variant and feature recognition, tool changer, data extraction, etc.) to explore the technologies that will be at the heart of our rapid value recovery disassembly setup in our newly established Remanufactory lab. And we look more than forward to keeping Mikkel as part of that story as the grant will allow him to further develop his alpha prototype during his MSc studies.

![Image alt](/images/BatchDisassembly_Mikkel.jpg) 
<p style="font-size:12px; "> To the left: a demonstration prototype for batch disassembly processes (from the BSc thesis) | to the right: the next alpha prototyping step </p> 

**Project:** Mikkels BSc project explores how semi-automatic processes can make remanufacturing smarter and more flexible. By combining simple mechanical fixtures with vision systems, force sensing, and modular software, a demonstrator was developed to handle batch-based disassembly of varying products. The work highlights the key trade-off between mechanical precision and software flexibility, showing how hybrid automation can support complex disassembly tasks while pointing the way toward more robust, scalable industrial solutions
